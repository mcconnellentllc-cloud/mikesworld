Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about ordering from Pasture Perfect Premium Beef.

Home / FAQ
How is your beef raised? +

Our Black Angus cattle are raised on open pastures here on our family farm in Sedgwick, Colorado. They bask in the Colorado sunshine before receiving a grain-finished diet that adds to the richness of flavor. We grow our own feed on the farm, reducing our carbon footprint and ensuring the highest quality nutrition for our cattle.

What does "dry-aged 21 days" mean? +

Dry aging is a process where beef is aged in a controlled, refrigerated environment for 21 days. During this time, natural enzymes break down the muscle tissue, resulting in significantly more tender and flavorful beef. This process concentrates the beef flavor and creates the melt-in-your-mouth texture that our customers love.

What's the difference between grain-finished and grass-finished? +

All our cattle start on pasture. Grain-finished cattle receive a diet of farm-grown grain in the final months, which produces rich marbling and a buttery flavor. Grass-finished cattle remain on pasture throughout, resulting in a leaner cut with a more robust, earthy flavor. Both options are excellent — it comes down to personal preference.

How much beef will I get with a Quarter, Half, or Whole order? +

A Whole Beef yields a minimum of 529 pounds of beef. A Half Beef is approximately half that amount, and a Quarter Beef provides a proportionate sample. All three options include a fine assortment of steaks, roasts, ground beef, and specialty cuts. We pride ourselves on providing more beef per order than the average supplier.

Can I choose my own cuts? +

Yes! Customers who choose Half or Whole portions can select their preferred cuts. You have the luxury of deciding how thick your steaks will be, the exact size of your roasts, and how many pounds each package of ground beef should contain. Specialty picks like Prime Rib, Brisket, and Organ Meat are also available. Quarter Beef customers receive cuts carefully selected to provide great variety and value.

Are processing fees included in the price? +

Yes! Unlike many other suppliers, our prices include all processing fees. We believe in transparent pricing with no hidden costs. What you see is what you pay — no surprises at pickup.

Is your beef USDA certified? +

Absolutely. All our beef is processed at USDA certified facilities, and each cut is meticulously inspected to meet the highest safety and quality standards. You can enjoy our beef with complete confidence.

What payment methods do you accept? +

We accept PayPal and Venmo for fast, secure, and convenient payments. PayPal allows you to pay with your PayPal balance, bank account, or credit/debit card. With Venmo, you can send payment directly from your Venmo account. Visit our Products page to place your order and pay online.

Do you ship your beef? +

Yes! Our ground beef ships conveniently to your door. For larger bulk orders (Quarter, Half, and Whole), please contact us to discuss pickup or delivery arrangements. We're always happy to work with our customers to find the best solution.

How do I store bulk beef? +

Bulk beef should be stored in a chest or upright freezer. A Quarter Beef typically requires about 4-5 cubic feet of freezer space, a Half Beef needs about 8 cubic feet, and a Whole Beef requires about 16 cubic feet. Properly frozen beef maintains excellent quality for 12 months or more.
